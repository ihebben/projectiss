Introduction

Graph theory is the study of graphs, which are mathematical structures that are used to modelize a situation using vertices and edges

the science of graph theory can be applied in many fields, ranging from chemistry (the structure of a molecule can be represented as a graph with the atoms being the vertices and the bonds being the edges) to energy.

The usage of graph theory in the field of energy engineering has been growing lately for many reasons. This is seen from the increase of shcolar papers that relate the two topics to help solve a problem. One of the main reasons for applying graph theory to renewable energies is that the latter relies heavily on efficiency and cost reduction. At the same time, the science of graph theory engulfs many algorithms and strategies whose objectives are mainly to visualize a problem and give an effecient and cost-friendly solution, and that is why the interconnectivity of graph theory with renewable energy engineering was bound to happen eventually.

In this website, we will be discussing two applications of graph theory in the field of energy, and more specifically, renewable energies. In this field, graph theory is used to increase efficiency and give simple representations of circuits.

    The applications we will be discussing are:
  • Application 1: Cost efficient way to connect wind turbines in a wind field
  • Application 2: Graph representation of Electrical networks

Cost Efficient inter-connection of wind turbines

On-shore wind farms are very large pieces of farm land where large scale wind turbines (140 meteres long with 55 meters blades) are positioned with the objective of produing electricity. The number of wind farms is gradually increasing nowadays, with the largest being Gansu wind farm in China, which has a capacity of 6 GW.

On shore wind farms have been criticized for their impact on the landscape: Wind turbines are very large compared to other power sources, which means wind farms have to cover a huge chunk of land, usually in rural areas.

This raises a separate type of problem for engineers. Since wind turbines in wind farms are not close together, their inter connectivity, and wiring, requires special attention because it becomes a costly matter if not taken into account. In fact, wind turbines have to be connected to the power station so their power can be harnessed, and in large fields such as wind farms, there are infinite ways to connect the wind turbines to each other and to the power station.

Therefore, the problem to solve becomes: How do we connect all wind turbines in a wind field to the power station in the most cost-efficient manner?

we will be using graph theory to solve this problem, and more specifically, trees: In graph theory, a tree is an undirected, connected and acyclic graph. In other words, a connected graph that does not contain even a single cycle is called a tree. So our objective becomes to find a tree structure that connects all our wind turbines with the smallest weight. To rephrase, we are looking for the minimum spanning tree.

The process of finding the minimum spanning tree for a wind farm is:

  1. Create your graph using the wind turbines as vertices. The distance between two wind turbines is the weight of the graph
  2. Create a complete graph (a graph where all the vertices are connected) where all the possibilities are present
  3. Apply Kruskal's algorithm on the complete graph
    • find the edge with the smallest weight
    • find the edge with the second smallest weight (it doesn't have to be connected to the first)
    • Repeat until all the vertices are covered. Make sure to skip any edge that creates a cycle within your graph
  4. the resultant tree is the minimum spanning tree. It is also the most cost-efficient way to connect the wind turbines to the power station

Graph Representation of electrical networks

In this application, we will link graph theory to the physics of electrical circuits. More specifically, we will use graphs to represent electrical networks. This strategy can assist us in the calculations we conduct on a circuit. For example, if we are interested in a mesh of a network, we can just take the subgraph of the equivalent graph that represents the mesh and apply the necessary maths on that unique part.

The problem to solve is: How can we use directed graphs to represent an electrical network?

We will start by defining some electrical network terms that we will need later to further understand the application

  • Branch: single path conataining one circuit element, which connects one node to any other node.
    In the graph representation, a branch is represented by an edge. The weight of the edge will be the reactance of the component.
  • Node: A point at which two or more circuit element have a common connection
    the number of branches incident to the node is known as the degree of the node. In our graph representation, we will use vertices to represent nodes.
  • Current direction: In an electrical circuit, the current follows a strict path according to the orientation of the generator. In our case, we will represent the current direction by using directed edges.

Before moving to the process, there is a couple of details that we have to keep in mind

  • the number of branches in a circuit will be equal to the number of vertices
  • there are multiple graph representations for the same circuit, as long as the topology of the network remains the same

It is important to know that there are different types of graph representations for electrical networks.For example, we can represent a circuit by a graph with labeled vertices. In this case, the label of the vertix corresponds to the voltage at the specific node (after the different voltage drops). Another possible example is representing the components as the vertices.

the process of finding the corresponding graph of an electrical circuit

  1. Start by drawing your vertices. You must have as much vertices as there are nodes in the circuit.
  2. Link the vertices using edges, each edge corresponds to an electric component.
    if there are two components in one branch, place an extra vertix (node) between them.
  3. for each component, calculate it's reactance. The reactance will correspond to the weight of the component
  4. Replace the sources by their internal resistance
  5. direct each edge according to the direction of the current in the original circuit

Conclusion

In conclusion, there are many applications of graph theory in the field of renewable energies, and we have seen two applications: the first application explains how we can use Kruskal's minimum spanning tree algorithm to connect wind turbines in a wind farm with the most cost efficient method, while the second developed on the representation of electrical circuits with directed graphs.
